About Me

I am a Computer Vision and Machine Learning researcher based in Espoo, Finland. I hold a Master of Science (MSc) degree in Software and Telecommunication Technology from the University of Jyväskylä, and I am currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Machine Learning and Computer Vision at Aalto University. My experience includes the development and implementation of custom detection solutions using computer vision techniques. I have specialized in image and video processing, building object detection and recognition solutions, predominantly leveraging deep learning methodologies. In my previous role as a Solution Consultant, I actively engaged in the development of Machine Learning and DevOps solutions for diverse customer use cases at Tietoevry. I am dedicated to advancing my knowledge and research pursuits in my current role as a Doctoral Researcher in Content-Based Image and Information Retrieval Group at Aalto University, Department of Computer Science.

My interest in science lies in researching and developing new innovative AI solutions to obtain information from images and videos. In my thesis I studied the sensitivity of pretrained lightweight object detection models against the adversarial noise in images. I chose this subject as it combined the security aspect and computer vision, which were two main study lines that I took courses during my studies. Prior to my thesis I worked as a Research Assistant in Faculty of Information Technology at University of Jyväskylä in Computer Vision Project which taught me the basics of research workflow. In future projects I am looking to integrate computer vision into sports and environmental studies to develop innovative solutions and acquire valuable insights through this technology. Currently in my research I am working with Remote Sensing, Hyperspectral Imaging, Computer Vision and Deep Learning.
